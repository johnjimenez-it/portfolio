Project 1: OmniLog - Your Ultimate Media Companion

OmniLog is a user-friendly and fully functional website that allows users to log and review various types of media including TV shows, movies, books, and music. The website enables users to connect with friends and share this information amongst each other.

  • Vision: Turning mindless asocial media consumption into a process that encourages self-reflection and connection with others.
  • Key Features: Centralized logging, user-friendly interface, social integration, personalized recommendations, and trending content.
  • Scope: Developed a user-friendly website that enables users to create accounts, log media consumption, and share these logs with friends.
  • Risk Assessment: Implemented security measures including vulnerability scanning, input sanitization, and DDoS prevention services.
  • Cost: Total budget of $125,000 covering development, testing, marketing, and deployment.
  • Communication Plan: Regular updates and feedback loops with stakeholders to ensure project goals are met.
  • Timeline: Completed in 7 months, including planning, development, testing, and deployment.

Project 2: Information Architecture for Tri-County Humane Society

This project involved evaluating and redesigning the information architecture of the Tri-County Humane Society website to improve usability and user experience. The redesign included a streamlined navigation system, better organization of key information, and enhanced visibility of important sections.

  • Purpose: Facilitate and encourage community engagement through volunteer opportunities and pet adoptions.
  • Improvements: Simplified header and footer, more efficient communication of announcements, better overall organization, and a site-wide search bar.
  • Personas: Developed detailed personas representing key user groups, including future pet parents, volunteers, supporters (donors), and those seeking pet health and loss information.
  • Wireframes: Created wireframes and prototypes to visualize and test the new design concepts.
  • Reflective Analysis: Applied the People-Content-Context Framework and IA principles to ensure the redesigned website met user needs and organizational goals.

Project 3: GetInvolved Website Promotion

This project focused on promoting the GetInvolved website to increase student engagement at FSU CCI. As the team leader, I guided our team in implementing various strategies, including flyer distribution and digital outreach, to boost traffic to the website.

  • Goals: Increase website traffic by 15-20% and engage more students in student organizations.
  • Results: Achieved a 42% increase in website traffic and exceeded flyer distribution goals.
  • Team Performance: Each team member contributed to the success by promoting the website and suggesting improvement ideas.
  • Leadership: Demonstrated effective leadership and teamwork, leading to significant project success.
  • Recommendations: Sustain engagement through continuous content updates and explore additional promotional channels.

Project 4: Robson College Website Redesign

This project aimed to improve the accessibility and engagement of the Robson College website. Our team conducted user research, designed a prototype, and proposed a budget to transform the website into a more user-friendly and efficient platform.

  • Problem Statement: Address poor user engagement and accessibility issues and improve the content management system's efficiency.
  • Survey Insights: Collected feedback from over 2750 stakeholders to identify areas for improvement.
  • Technology Stack: Utilized WordPress with specific plugins, Microsoft hosting services, and security measures.
  • Prototype Demonstration: Developed a prototype incorporating advanced search functionalities and adaptable design.
  • Budget Breakdown: Detailed budget covering software, hardware, hosting, and labor costs.
  • Projected Outcomes: Expected a 30% improvement in user engagement and a 20% increase in new student applications.
